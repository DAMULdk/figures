Square

A square is a four-sided polygon with equal sides and equal angles. It is a special case of a rectangle where all four sides are of equal length. The angles of a square are all right angles (90 degrees), and opposite sides are parallel. The diagonals of a square bisect each other at right angles, dividing the square into four congruent right triangles. The perimeter of a square is the sum of the lengths of its four sides, while its area is calculated by squaring the length of one of its sides.

Triangle

A triangle is a polygon with three sides and three angles. It's classified based on the lengths of its sides and the measures of its angles. Types include equilateral (all sides and angles are equal), isosceles (two sides and angles are equal), and scalene (all sides and angles are different). The area of a triangle can be calculated using different methods, and its perimeter is the sum of the lengths of its sides.

Circle

A circle is a two-dimensional shape with all points on its boundary equidistant from its center. It has a radius, which is the distance from the center to any point on the circle's edge. Circles are fundamental geometric shapes found in nature and commonly used in mathematics, art, and engineering for their symmetry and simplicity.

Rectangle

A rectangle is a quadrilateral with four right angles (90-degree angles). Opposite sides of a rectangle are equal in length, and its diagonals are equal in length and bisect each other. The area of a rectangle can be calculated by multiplying its length by its width, while its perimeter is the sum of all four sides. Rectangles are commonly encountered in everyday life and are used in architecture , design, and various mathematical applications.

Cube

A cube is a three-dimensional geometric shape with six square faces of equal size. All angles within a cube are right angles, and all edges are of equal length. The cube is a regular polyhedron, meaning all of its faces are congruent and its angles are equal. Cubes are widely used in architecture, engineering, and mathematics due to their regularity and symmetry.

Sphere

A sphere is a three-dimensional object with all points on its surface equidistant from its center. It is perfectly symmetrical, with no edges or vertices. The distance from the center to any point on the surface is called the radius. Spheres are commonly encountered in nature and are used in various fields such as physics, astronomy, and engineering for their uniformity and simplicity of shape.

Right prism

A right prism is a three-dimensional geometric figure characterized by parallel congruent polygonal bases connected by rectangular faces. Each vertex of the base is connected to the corresponding vertex of the other base by a perpendicular edge. The height of the prism is the perpendicular distance between the two bases. Right prisms include rectangular prisms, triangular prisms, hexagonal prisms, and so on, depending on the shape of their bases. They are widely used in geometry and engineering for their regularity and ease of calculation.

Tesseract

A tesseract, or hypercube, is a four-dimensional geometric figure analogous to a cube in three dimensions. It consists of eight cubical cells connected in a manner similar to how a cube is formed by connecting six square faces. Beyond three dimensions, mathematicians explore higher-dimensional shapes such as hyperspheres and hypercubes. These concepts, while abstract, have significant applications in mathematics, physics, and computer science.
