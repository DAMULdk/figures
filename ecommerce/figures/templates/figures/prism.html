Prism: Definition and Properties

A prism is a three-dimensional geometric figure with two congruent polygonal bases and rectangular sides. When the cross-section of the prism is a polygon, and the side faces are perpendicular to the base, it is called a right prism.

Calculations for a Prism

To calculate various properties of a prism:

  • Number of Sides (n): The number of sides in the base polygon of the prism.
  • Side Length (s): The length of each side of the base polygon.
  • Height (h): The perpendicular distance between the two bases of the prism.
  • Apothem Length (a): The apothem is the distance from the center of the base polygon to the midpoint of any side. It's also the radius of the inscribed circle.

  • Lateral Surface Area: The lateral surface area of a prism is given by the formula: A = n * s * h, where n is the number of sides, s is the length of each side of the base polygon, and h is the height of the prism.
  • Base Surface Area: The base surface area of a prism is given by the formula: Abase = 0.5 * n * s * a, where a is the apothem length.
  • Vertex Count: The number of vertices in a prism is given by the formula: V = 2n, where n is the number of sides of the base polygon.
  • Edge Count: The number of edges in a prism is given by the formula: E = 3n, where n is the number of sides of the base polygon.

Fun Fact about Prisms

Prisms are commonly found in architecture and engineering as they are used in the construction of buildings, bridges, and other structures. They play a significant role in providing support and stability to various constructions.
