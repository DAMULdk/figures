Square: Definition and Properties

A square is a geometric shape characterized by four equal sides and four right angles. It is a special case of a rectangle where all sides are of equal length.

Calculations for a Square

To calculate various properties of a square:

  • Side Length: Since all sides of a square are equal, the side length can be determined by measuring any one side.
  • Area: The area of a square is found by squaring the length of one side. Mathematically, it can be expressed as: A = s2, where s represents the length of a side.
  • Perimeter: The perimeter of a square is the sum of the lengths of all four sides. It can be calculated as: P = 4s, where s represents the length of a side.
  • Diagonal: The diagonal of a square is a line segment connecting two opposite vertices. It divides the square into two congruent right triangles. The length of the diagonal can be calculated using the Pythagorean theorem: d = √(s2 + s2) = √2s.

Fun Fact about Squares

A square is a symmetric shape, meaning it looks the same when rotated by 90, 180, or 270 degrees. This property makes it a fundamental element in various mathematical and architectural designs, symbolizing stability and equality.
